Traditional Bengali Rosogulla

Bangladeshi rosogulla is a beloved, iconic sweet made from soft, spongy dumplings of fresh chhena (cottage cheese) and semolina, cooked in light, fragrant sugar syrup. Deeply rooted in Bengali culture as a symbol of hospitality and joy, this delicacy is characterized by its light, juicy texture and is often served during celebrations and festivals.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups milk
  • ¼ cup lemon juice
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • 4 ½ cups water

Instructions

Making Paneer

  1. Mix lemon juice in half cup of hot water and keep aside.
  2. Boil the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent burning. As the milk boils, gradually add the lemon juice and stir gently. The curd will start separating from the whey. Turn off the heat.
  3. Once the milk fat has separated from the whey, drain the whey using a strainer lined with cheesecloth or muslin cloth. Wrap the curd in the cloth, rinse under cold water, and squeeze well to remove sourness from the lemon.
  4. Remove excess water by pressing the wrapped paneer under a heavy pan for about one hour. Check moisture by rubbing a small piece in your palm; it should form a firm but smooth ball.
  5. Place the paneer on a clean surface and knead for 3-4 minutes until it forms a smooth soft dough. Knead by dragging your palm over it, scooping back, and adding a teaspoon of water if too crumbly.

Making Rasgulla

  1. Divide the paneer dough into 12 equal parts and roll into smooth balls, applying pressure then releasing while shaping.
  2. Mix sugar and water in a pressure cooker on medium-high heat and bring to a boil.
  3. Add the paneer balls and close the pressure cooker. Once it starts steaming, reduce heat to medium and cook for about 7 minutes.
  4. Ensure the pressure cooker is large enough; Rosogulla expand to about double in volume during cooking.
  5. Turn off heat and let it rest for a few minutes before opening. Pour cold water over the cooker before opening to cool.
  6. Rosogulla should be slightly spongy. After refrigeration, they remain soft but slightly less spongy.
  7. Serve chilled and enjoy!

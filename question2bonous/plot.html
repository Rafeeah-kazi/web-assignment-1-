Plot Summary

Aspiring journalist Andrea "Andy" Sachs graduates from Northwestern University and moves to New York City to pursue a career in journalism. Despite having no knowledge of fashion, she is hired as a junior assistant to Miranda Priestly, the powerful and demanding editor-in-chief of Runway magazine.

Andy initially struggles to fit in with her stylish co-workers, especially Miranda’s senior assistant, Emily Charlton. She finds it difficult to meet Miranda’s impossible expectations and endures constant criticism. After failing to arrange a flight during a hurricane, Andy seeks advice from art director Nigel, who helps her transform her appearance and better understand the fashion world.

As Andy becomes more confident and fashionable, Miranda begins assigning her more important responsibilities. This creates tension with Emily, who hopes to attend Paris Fashion Week but falls ill due to extreme dieting. After Andy saves Miranda from embarrassment at a charity event, Miranda chooses Andy to accompany her to Paris instead of Emily.

In Paris, Andy discovers that Miranda’s husband has filed for divorce. She also learns that Miranda may be replaced by Jacqueline Follet. Although Andy attempts to warn Miranda, Miranda reveals she already knows and strategically sacrifices Nigel’s career opportunity to secure her own position.

Realizing she is becoming someone she does not want to be, Andy decides to leave. She throws her phone into the Fontaine de la Concorde and walks away from Miranda.

Back in New York, Andy reconnects with her boyfriend Nate and interviews for a journalism job at the New York Mirror. Although Miranda describes Andy as her “biggest disappointment,” she recommends hiring her, indirectly showing respect for Andy’s abilities.

After securing the job, Andy moves forward with her career. In the final scene, she sees Miranda, who does not acknowledge her publicly but smiles privately, suggesting mutual understanding and respect.
